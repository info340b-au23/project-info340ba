LOOKING FOR INTERNSHIPS?

Here's a student-advice guide on how to get your foot in the door and build valuable experience for internships and your major application process!

WHERE DO I FIND OPPORTUNITIES?

1. Career Fairs

Going to a career fair can be one of the best experiences you can do in your undergraduate career. You get the opportunity to meet and connect with hiring managers and recruiters that are currently in the industry. Career fairs are one of the best ways to make connections with recruiters in-person.

2. School Clubs

Joining school clubs are a great way to meet with other students and create new connections. Depending on the club, you might also be able to meet with mentors that can help you in your major application and resumes. Here is the link to UW's official RSO directory

3. Professors

Don't be afriad to connect with your professors. They can provide valuable assistance and resources on your internship journey. Professors possess extensive networks and resources that they are often willing to leverage for your benefit.

4. Job Listing Sites*

Job listing websites streamline students' internship searches, offering a centralized platform to find and apply for relevant opportunities, simplifying the process, and facilitating professional growth. We have listed some websites that you can leverage to aid in your internship search.

JOB LISTING WEBSITES

1. Linkedin

LinkedIn is a global professional networking platform where individuals and businesses connect, showcasing their skills and experience. It's a valuable resource for job hunting, networking, and industry insights.

2. Handshake

Handshake is a platform connecting students and recent graduates to job and internship opportunities, offering a user-friendly interface and tailored recommendations or both job seekers and employers.

3. Simplify

Simplify is a platform that allows users to efficiently search for open internship and job positions.

4. Indeed

Indeed is another platform where users are able to job search.
